About me

My Research

My research focuses on understanding high-redshift galaxy formation and evolution, with particular emphasis on the circumgactic gas and its link with the galaxies' properties. The discovery of very extended Lyman-alpha nebulae around high-redshift quasars is routinely reported nowadays, opening up the study of halo gas around massive systems and allowing the characterization of the atomic gas properties. On the contrary, little is known about the molecular gas reservoirs in these galaxies. In this context, I have been working at the interface between observations and simulations. With observations I constrained the molecular gas reservoirs in quasars' host galaxies at z~3, and with simulations I predicted [CII] emission levels in halo scales of z~4-6 star-forming galaxies.

Additionally, I have also experience working with local objects and optical data. During my master's studies I performed a spectroscopic and kinematic analysis of a nearby interacting system of galaxies, determining their main physical properties.
